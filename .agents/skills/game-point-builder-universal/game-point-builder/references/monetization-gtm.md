## Comp table

This category already exists and already monetizes — GamePoint is entering a validated market, not creating one. Positioning should differentiate on breadth (any title, via title-agnostic screen-vision, vs. most comps' single-title depth) and on the PiP-native, truth-bar coaching discipline (§V of SKILL.md), not on "first mover."

| Product | Platform | Scope | Modality | Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Questie.ai | Standalone | Cross-title | Screen vision + voice + "personality" companion | $19.99/mo | Closest direct comp to GamePoint's described concept — screen vision, voice, PiP-adjacent companion framing |
| STATUP.GG | Overwolf | League of Legends only | Voice coaching, 4 verbosity modes | Free (ad-supported) | Deep single-title depth; publisher-tolerated via Overwolf's platform relationship |
| Mobalytics / Porofessor | Overwolf | MOBA titles | Overlay stats + build/timer guidance | Freemium | Overwolf-class precedent for "tolerated overlay" |
| NVIDIA Project G-Assist | RTX-hardware-bound | Cross-app/game | Voice-activated local SLM, performance + light gameplay help | Free (RTX GPU required) | First-party hardware vendor position — no ToS friction with publishers since it's system-level, not game-injected |
| Microsoft Gaming Copilot | Xbox Game Bar / Xbox app | Cross-title | Chat/voice overlay, coaching-style tips | Free (bundled) | First-party platform position — same durable-moat argument as references/legal-guardrails.md's "publisher partnership" recommendation |

## Pricing bands

The validated band across direct comps is **$5–$20/mo**, freemium-gated (STATUP free/ad-supported at the low end, Questie.ai at $19.99/mo at the high end for the closest-matching feature set). Recommend anchoring GamePoint's paid tier inside that band rather than pricing above the category ceiling without a demonstrated reason (e.g., cross-title breadth alone likely isn't enough differentiation to price above Questie.ai until the knowledge-pack depth matches single-title specialists).

## Positioning notes

- **Against Questie.ai**: match on screen-vision + voice + companion framing; differentiate on truth-bar discipline (§V — every claim traceable to the knowledge pack, explicit "not sure" over invented specifics) and on the compliance-first multi-title expansion model (§IV), which de-risks GamePoint's growth into higher-scrutiny titles in a way an ad-hoc competitor may not have documented.
- **Against Overwolf-class single-title tools**: match on depth per title only after the per-title knowledge pack matures; lead with breadth (any title, not just League/Valorant-adjacent titles) as the wedge.
- **Against first-party tools (G-Assist, Gaming Copilot)**: these aren't really competitors so much as evidence the category is safe and mainstream — cite them in any compliance/investor conversation as proof that "AI watches your screen and coaches you" is an accepted product category at the platform-vendor level, not just an indie gray-area play.
